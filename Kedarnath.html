Explore : Kedaranath

Kedarnath Temple (Sanskrit: केदारनाथ मंदिर, IAST: Kēdāranātha Mandira, lit. 'temple of the God of the field') is a Hindu temple, one of the twelve jyotirlinga of Shiva. The temple is located on the Garhwal Himalayan range[citation needed] near the Mandakini river, in the state of Uttarakhand, India. Due to extreme weather conditions, the temple is open to the general public only between the months of April (Akshaya Tritiya) and November (Kartik Purnima, the autumn full moon). During the winters, the vigraha (deity) of the temple is carried down to Ukhimath to be worshipped for the next six months. Kedarnath is seen as a homogeneous form of Shiva, the 'Lord of Kedarkhand', the historical name of the region.[1]

The temple is not directly accessible by road and has to be reached by a 22 kilometres (14 mi) uphill trek from Gaurikund. Pony, mule and manchan service is available to reach the temple. According to Hindu legends, the temple was initially built by the Pandavas, and is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, the holiest Hindu shrines of Shiva.[2]

Kadaranath : An Overview




Kedarnath is a town and Nagar Panchayat in Rudraprayag district of Uttarakhand, India, known primarily for the Kedarnath Temple. It is approximately 86 kilometres from Rudraprayag, the district headquarter. Kedarnath is the most remote of the four Chota Char Dham pilgrimage sites.

It is located in the Himalayas, about 3,583 m (11,755 ft) above sea level near the Chorabari Glacier, which is the source of the Mandakini river. The town is flanked by snow-capped peaks, most prominently the Kedarnath Mountain. The nearest road head is at Gaurikund about 16 km away. The town suffered extensive destruction during June 2013 from the Flash Floods caused by torrential rains in Uttarakhand state.[4]



How to Reach : Kadaranath



Before you embark on your Trip to Kadaranath, planning your trip carefully is essential. Here are some important things you should consider way to reach Kadarnath .Hope its going to help you :

  1. By Air : The most expedient and efficient way of getting to the auspicious destination of Kedarnath is via air. Due to the altitude and location of the state of Uttarakhand, the Char Dham Yatra pilgrimage can be quite strenuous. This means one would most likely want to reach the base camp of Gaurikund as quickly as possible, reserving all energy for the uphill trek to Kedarnath.
    While Kedarnath doesn’t operate its own aerodrome, individuals can avail of the nearest air hub in Dehradun. Jolly Grant Airport is situated approximately 238 to 250 km from Kedarnath, playing a pivotal role in Uttarakhand tourism. The airport is well connected to major cities such as Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Chennai, Kolkata and Lucknow. Individuals may select from the following carriers – IndiGo, Spice Jet and Air India. In terms of the fastest route, New Delhi’s Indira Gandhi International airport is only 260 km away, offering a flight duration of 45 minutes to an hour. Upon arrival, individuals may select from general, prepaid and private taxis as well as the city bus service to reach Kedarnath.
    Jolly Grant Airport is equipped with all the necessary facilities, including high-speed WiFi, infotainment, buffet and bar services. This means travellers can breathe a sigh of relief as their arrival will be met with a host of amenities.

  2. By Road : In order to get to Kedarnath by road, travellers will have to get to the capital city of Delhi first. From Delhi, one can reach Dehradun by flight, train or bus. It is from Dehradun that you will be making your way to Haridwar and from this sacred city you will be heading for the path that leads to the Kedarnath trek. There are two major interstate bus depots from Delhi that schedule regular buses to Dehradun, namely - Kashmere Gate Bus Terminal and Anand Vihar Bus Terminal. Individuals may select from A/C sleeper, non-A/C sleeper, Volvo A/C, Volvo A/C and semi-sleeper buses, depending upon their needs.
    We have chalked out a rough road route for you to get a sense of the magnitude of the journey: New Delhi > Haridwar (206 km) > Rishikesh (24 km) > Devprayag (74 km) > Srinagar (34 km) > Rudraprayag (33 km) > Gaurikund (via Augustmuni > Guptkashi > Phata > Sitapur > Sonprayag (74 km) > Kedarnath by trek (16 km).
    Take note that this is a long and exhausting commute. We’d recommend getting to Gaurikund as quickly as possible, as the trip ahead is even more physically taxing.

  3. By Train : For those looking for a more cost-effective transportation medium, there are options available to you. However, bear in mind that this increases the duration of your commute significantly as the destination is a starting point to another journey itself.
    Although there isn’t a railway junction in Kedarnath, individuals can avail of the railhead at Rishikesh. Situated approximately 210 km from Gaurikund, Rishikesh Railway Station is well connected to the capital city. Individuals may hop onto a fast train from Delhi to Rishikesh, which will take approximately 8 hours in duration. Upon arrival, there are regular buses that ply to the base camp, from which the trek begins.
